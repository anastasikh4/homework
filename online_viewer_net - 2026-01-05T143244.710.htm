Prepositions: In, On, At, To

Quick Rule: Use In for months/seasons/parts of day; On for days/dates; At for times/specific places; To for movement.
1 I take a shower the morning.
2 We usually go on vacation July.
3 My cousin studies engineering university.
4 The flight arrives midnight.
5 Do they stay a hotel or an apartment?
6 She has meetings Mondays and Wednesdays.
7 My colleagues have coffee work.
8 I don't like working the weekend.
9 Mark goes the office by train.
10 The park is beautiful the winter.
